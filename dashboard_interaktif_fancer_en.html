Business Insights & Rekomendasi
01
High Priority
Default Rate 21.7% — Jauh di Atas Normal
1 in 5 customers defaulted on their first installment. A healthy multifinance NPL target is <5%. This portfolio reflects a high-risk segment.
→ Conduct a comprehensive review of the credit scoring and approval process
02
Insight
Employment Type Is Not the Primary Risk Driver
Self-employed 22.69% vs Salaried 20.35% — only a 2.3% gap. Not significant enough to be the primary credit approval filter.
→ Combine with credit score & LTV for more accurate approval decisions
03
Risk Factor
LTV 75–90% Is the Riskiest and Largest Segment
The 75–90% LTV segment has a 24.65% default rate and dominates with 126,463 customers (54% of portfolio). High volume + high risk combination.
→ Tighten minimum down payment requirements for customers with LTV above 75%
04
Risk Factor
Young Customers (<25 Years) Are Most Vulnerable
24.1% default rate for age <25 vs 19.8% for age 45+. Consistent trend — younger customers carry higher risk.
→ Customers under 25 must provide a guarantor or co-borrower
05
Critical
50% of Customers Have No Credit History
116,950 customers (50.1%) have no credit history at all, with a 23.1% default rate — equivalent to medium-high risk customers.
→ Unscored customers should be treated as medium-high risk, not ignored
06
Anomali
Category A (Very Low Risk) — Underperforms Expectations
Highest score range (806–890) has a 16.57% default rate — higher than C (17.26%) and nearly equal to E (17.18%). Requires further investigation.
→ Re-audit the classification parameters for Category A in the CIBIL system
